International Career

First Steps into Cricket

Virat Kohli began his cricket journey in Delhi at the West Delhi Cricket Academy, quickly rising through age-group levels with standout performances. In 2006, he displayed remarkable grit by scoring 90 in a Ranji match just a day after losing his father — a powerful glimpse into his unwavering determination and love for the game.

A Rookie with Fire

After making his mark for Delhi, Virat Kohli's consistent domestic form earned him a spot in India's U-19 squad. His talent and maturity stood out, and by 2008, he was leading the side to a U-19 World Cup win — a defining moment that showcased his leadership and set the stage for his international debut later that year.

Breaking into Team India

In 2006, he displayed remarkable grit by scoring 90 in a Ranji match just a day after losing his father — a powerful glimpse into his unwavering determination and love for the game.

International Breakthrough

Virat Kohli's breakthrough came in the 2009 Champions Trophy, where he stepped up in Yuvraj Singh's absence. His unbeaten 79* against West Indies under pressure proved his big-match temperament and marked the arrival of a future middle-order mainstay.

Among Legends

In the 2011 World Cup, as Sachin Tendulkar chased his lifelong dream, a young Virat Kohli stood beside him — not just as a teammate, but as a passionate admirer. Contributing with grit and composure, Virat helped realize a nation's dream while quietly beginning his journey toward cricketing greatness.

Fearless Captain

In December 2014, after MS Dhoni retired from Tests mid-series in Australia, Virat Kohli was handed full-time captaincy. Having already led in Dhoni's absence, he took charge for the Sydney Test — marking the start of a bold, aggressive new era in Indian Test cricket.

Rise of Virat's ERA

After the legendary Sachin era, Virat Kohli emerged as the new face of Indian cricket. Where Sachin embodied grace and precision, Kohli brought fire, relentless consistency, and a revolution in fitness. He didn't just follow in Sachin's footsteps — he carved his own path, redefining excellence and leading India into a fearless, dominant era.

Leading from the front

Since taking over as Test captain in 2014, Virat Kohli transformed India into a world-beating side. With bold leadership, a sharp focus on fitness, and an aggressive mindset, he led India to the No. 1 ranking, a historic series win in Australia, and built one of the most formidable pace attacks in Test history.

All format captain

In January 2017, after MS Dhoni stepped down, Virat Kohli took charge as ODI and T20 captain. With his trademark intensity and consistency, he led India to the top rankings, dominated bilateral series, and guided a strong 2019 World Cup campaign. His aggressive leadership redefined India's approach in limited-overs cricket.

Conquering the world

Virat Kohli thrived in SENA countries, scoring centuries in all four and delivering standout knocks, especially in England (2018) and Australia. As captain, he led India to a historic Test series win in Australia and fiercely competed in South Africa, England, and New Zealand — earning global acclaim for his grit and leadership.

Royal Challengers Bangalore

Ipl Debut

In 2008, a young Virat Kohli was picked by Royal Challengers Bangalore for the inaugural IPL season after leading India to a U-19 World Cup win. Though his early years were modest, he showed glimpses of his potential — marking the beginning of a legendary journey with RCB.

Young gun

After making his mark for Delhi, Virat Kohli's consistent domestic form earned him a spot in India's U-19 squad. His talent and maturity stood out, and by 2008, he was leading the side to a U-19 World Cup win — a defining moment that showcased his leadership and set the stage for his international debut later that year.

Rcb captain

Virat Kohli's rise with RCB truly began in 2013 when he became full-time captain. He consistently led from the front with the bat, but his peak came in 2016, scoring a record-breaking 973 runs with 4 centuries, setting a new benchmark in IPL history.

Dream begins

Virat Kohli led RCB through mixed fortunes. The team struggled in 2014 despite his steady batting, finishing outside the playoffs. In 2015, they showed improvement, reaching the playoffs with Kohli anchoring the innings and forming a lethal duo with AB de Villiers building strong momentum for the franchise.

Tragedy of 2016

Virat Kohli had a historic season with 973 runs, leading RCB to the final. With AB de Villiers firing too, the title seemed within reach. But despite a strong chase, they fell short by just 8 runs against SRH — turning a dream season into heartbreak.

Rollercoster

RCB hit a rough patch during these years. In 2017, they finished at the bottom, with injuries and poor form derailing their season. 2018 brought a newlook squad, but inconsistency cost them a playoff spot. 2019 was another low, ending again at the bottom as issues with team balance and death bowling remained unsolved — making it a frustrating period for Kohli and fans alike.

RCB's Revival Phase

After the COVID break, RCB found renewed form under Kohli, consistently reaching the playoffs with a stronger, more balanced and competitive squad that showed promise and resilience, but the elusive title still remained out of reach.

The Season of Faith

The 2024 season was a fairytale ride for RCB. After losing 7 of their first 8 matches, they staged a stunning comeback by winning the next 6 in a row to reach the playoffs — but fell short of the title, ending a brave campaign without the trophy.

Ee Sala Cup Namdu!

RCB finally ended their long title drought in 2025, lifting their maiden IPL trophy. With Rajat Patidar as captain and Kohli leading with the bat, they played fearless cricket. Finishing second in the league, they powered through the playoffs and edged past Punjab Kings in a thrilling final — sealing a historic and emotional win.

Clutch

MCG Magic

In the 2022 T20 World Cup, India were 31/4 chasing 160 against Pakistan. Kohli's unbeaten 82, including a legendary straight six off Haris Rauf, showcased unreal composure under pressure. India won by 4 wickets in what's hailed as the greatest T20I knock ever.

Hobart Hurricane

In the 2012 Commonwealth Series,An tri series tournament, India had to chase 321 in just 40 overs to stay alive. Kohli smashed a stunning 133*, tearing into Malinga for 24 in an over, and sealed the win in just 36.4 overs. A ruthless, iconic chase masterclass.

The Mohali Miracle

In the 2016 T20 World Cup vs Australia, India were 49/3 chasing 161. Kohli rose with a stunning 82 under pressure, guiding India to victory and into the semifinals. A chasing masterclass praised by legends

Best in the test

At Edgbaston 2018, with India 100/5, Kohli fought back with a gritty 149. Battling swing and critics, he dragged India to 274, proving his class in tough English conditions—even in a narrow loss.

183 of Pure Domination

In the 2012 Asia Cup, chasing 330 vs Pakistan, Kohli smashed a breathtaking 183 after an early setback. With key partnerships and fearless strokeplay, he tore through the attack, leading India to a memorable win and his highest ODI score.

Adelaide Audacity

In the 2014 Adelaide Test, Kohli's captaincy debut, he led a bold chase of 364 with a fearless 141. Though India fell short, his knock signaled the start of a fiery new era.

Cape Town Conquered

In Cape Town 2018, Virat Kohli scored a valiant 153 against South Africa, standing tall while others crumbled. On a tough pitch, he fought alone, showing class, grit, and unmatched determination—truly conquering Cape Town with his bat.

1020 Days Later

In the 2022 Asia Cup vs Afghanistan, Kohli ended a 1020-day century drought with an emotional unbeaten 100—his first in T20Is. After intense scrutiny and setbacks, he roared back in style. The King was back.

Carried in the Final

In the 2014 T20 World Cup Final, Kohli scored a valiant 77 off 58, carrying India with over half the team's runs. Though India lost, his clutch performance earned him Player of the Tournament with 319 runs at an average of 106.

King's Last Dance

In his final T20I, Kohli scored a clutch 76 in the 2024 World Cup Final, lifting India from 34/3 to 176. Named Player of the Match, he sealed a 7-run win over South Africa and ended India's 11-year ICC trophy drought—signing off as the ultimate big-game legend.
